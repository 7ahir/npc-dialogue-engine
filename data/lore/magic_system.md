# The Magic of Aethermoor

## Aether — The Foundation

Aether is the raw substance of magic. It flows through ley lines beneath the earth's surface, much like water flows through underground rivers. Ley lines form a network that spans the entire continent, with major junctions — called nexus points — where multiple lines converge.

Aether is invisible to normal sight but can be perceived by trained individuals as a faint shimmer or warmth. Animals are naturally sensitive to aether flows: birds align their migration paths along ley lines, and cats are drawn to nexus points.

In its natural state, aether is neutral and harmless. It becomes dangerous when concentrated, disrupted, or channeled improperly. The Sundering was caused by an attempt to redirect the entire Ley Network, releasing catastrophic amounts of uncontrolled aether.

## The Ley Network

The First Peoples built the Ley Network over centuries, installing crystalline relay stations at nexus points to regulate and direct aether flow. At its peak, the Network powered an advanced civilization: healing chambers, communication arrays, climate regulation, and transportation gates.

The Sundering damaged approximately 70% of the Network. The remaining 30% functions at reduced capacity. Some sections — like the one beneath Ashenmoor — still pulse with measurable aether flow. Others have gone dark entirely.

Restoring the Network would require: locating intact relay crystals (like those in the Shattered Spire), mapping the surviving ley lines, and possessing the engineering knowledge to reconnect junctions. This knowledge exists in fragments across various Archives.

## Practical Magic in the Current Age

Without the full Ley Network, practical magic is limited. Most magical effects are achieved through aether crystals — storing ambient aether and releasing it in controlled bursts. This is closer to engineering than the wizardry of old.

Common magical applications:
- **Aether lamps**: Small crystals that emit light when activated by touch
- **Preservation wards**: Crystal arrays that slow decay in food storage
- **Communication stones**: Paired crystals that transmit voice over short distances (range: approximately 5 kilometers)
- **Healing salves**: Alchemical preparations infused with aether that accelerate natural healing

Advanced magical applications (rare, requires significant knowledge):
- **Wards**: Protective barriers maintained by large crystals at ley junctions
- **Scrying**: Using aether crystals at nexus points to perceive distant locations
- **Forging**: Infusing materials with aether during the smithing process (Starfall Steel)

## Shadow Magic

Shadow magic draws power not from aether but from the Shadow Plane — a parallel dimension that partially overlaps with the physical world in areas damaged by the Sundering. Shadow magic is easier to access than aether-based magic (it requires no crystals or ley lines) but is inherently corrupting.

Shadow magic effects include:
- **Shadow stepping**: Moving through shadows to travel short distances
- **Darkness manipulation**: Creating zones of absolute darkness
- **Shadow binding**: Trapping entities in their own shadows
- **Summoning**: Drawing shadow-creatures from the Shadow Plane

The Shadow Cult uses shadow magic. Their power has grown as the breach between planes has widened. Eldris believes the breach is widening because someone — likely the Cult — is destabilizing the remaining ley line junctions beneath Ashenmoor.
