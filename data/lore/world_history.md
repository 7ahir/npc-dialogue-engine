# The World of Aethermoor — A History

## The Age of Binding (circa 3,000 years ago)

The world was once a single, unified landmass called Pangaethera. The First Peoples discovered that aether — a raw magical substance flowing through ley lines beneath the earth — could be shaped by will and intent. They built the Ley Network, a system of crystalline conduits that channeled aether across the continent. The Network powered their cities, healed their sick, and held the fabric of reality together.

The First Accord was signed between the five original peoples: Humans, Dwarves, Elves, the Scaled Folk, and the Veilborn. Each people was given stewardship of a segment of the Ley Network. For a thousand years, this arrangement held.

## The Sundering (circa 2,000 years ago)

The Sundering was the catastrophic event that shattered Pangaethera into the current continents and islands. It was caused by the Veiled War — a conflict between the Veilborn and an alliance of the other four peoples. The Veilborn sought to redirect the entire Ley Network to fuel a ritual that would merge the physical world with the Shadow Plane, granting them dominion over both.

The ritual was interrupted but not stopped. The resulting magical backlash tore the land apart, creating the Shattered Sea and the Frozen Wastes. Thousands perished. The Veilborn were scattered and went into hiding. The Ley Network was damaged beyond full repair, though fragments still function.

The Sundering left permanent scars on the world. Wild magic zones — areas where aether leaks uncontrolled — appear near damaged ley line junctions. The Shadow Plane, partially merged during the interrupted ritual, bleeds through in certain locations, creating the Shadowtouched Lands.

## The Reconstruction (circa 500-1,500 years ago)

In the centuries following the Sundering, the surviving peoples rebuilt. Ashenmoor was founded by dwarven miners who discovered rich mineral deposits and a partially intact section of the Ley Network beneath the mountains. The town grew around the mines and became a trading hub.

The Archive was established beneath Ashenmoor's temple to preserve knowledge of the old world. Its keepers — scholars who swore to remain neutral in all conflicts — maintained records of the Ley Network, the First Accord, and the events of the Sundering.

## The Current Age

Ashenmoor is now a prosperous but isolated mountain town. The mines still produce iron, silver, and rare Moonstone. The Archive still stands, though fewer people visit it. The Shadow Cult — a group that seeks to complete the Veilborn's interrupted ritual — has grown bolder in recent years.

The Ley Network beneath Ashenmoor still pulses faintly. Scholars believe it could be restored, but doing so would require rare materials and knowledge that may have been lost in the Sundering.
