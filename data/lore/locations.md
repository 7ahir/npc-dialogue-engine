# Notable Locations

## Ashenmoor

A mountain town of roughly 3,000 residents built into the slopes of Mount Cinder. The town is arranged in tiers: the mines and forges at the base, residential areas in the middle, and the temple and Archive at the summit. The Ember Flagon tavern sits at the central crossroads where all tiers connect.

Ashenmoor's climate is cool and often misty. The forges produce a constant haze of smoke and ember, giving the town its name. The smell of hot metal and woodsmoke permeates everything.

## The Frozen Mines

A network of deep tunnels extending beneath Mount Cinder and into the Frostpeak range. The upper levels produce iron and silver. The lower levels, accessible only to experienced Guild miners, contain Moonstone deposits. The deepest tunnels are permanently frozen — not from altitude, but from proximity to a damaged ley line junction that radiates cold instead of heat.

The Frozen Mines are dangerous. Beyond the cold, miners report disorientation, time distortion, and auditory hallucinations in the deepest sections. Three miners have gone missing in the past year. The Guild has restricted access to the lower levels, but Moonstone demand keeps pushing miners deeper.

## The Ember Flagon

Mira Hearthstone's tavern, the social center of Ashenmoor. A two-story stone building with a massive fireplace that never goes out (rumored to be fed by a minor ley line tap). The ground floor is a common room with long tables. The upper floor has six private rooms for travelers.

The cellar is older than the tavern itself — carved from the mountain centuries ago. Mira uses it for ale storage, but there are sealed passages that lead deeper. The Shadow Cult has been using one of these passages for meetings, unaware that Mira has noticed.

## The Ashenmoor Archive

A vast underground library beneath the abandoned temple at Ashenmoor's summit. The Archive extends seven levels deep into the mountain. The upper three levels contain general knowledge: history, geography, natural philosophy. The lower four levels contain restricted texts on magic, the Ley Network, the Sundering, and the Shadow Plane.

Access to the lower levels requires the Keeper's permission. Eldris guards these texts carefully, not from secrecy but from safety — some texts are imbued with residual aether and can cause harm to unprepared readers.

## The Shattered Spire

A ruined tower on the ridge east of Ashenmoor. Before the Sundering, it was a ley line relay station. The Sundering broke it into three pieces that still stand at odd angles, defying gravity. Wild magic is strong here — plants grow in impossible patterns, stones float, and compasses spin.

The Spire contains crystalline components from the original Ley Network. Eldris believes one of these crystals could be used to repair the ward protecting the Archive from Shadow Plane intrusion. Reaching it requires climbing through the wild magic zone, which is disorienting and occasionally dangerous.

## Willowmere

A farming village two days' travel south of Ashenmoor. Connected by a single road through the Greenveil Forest. The road has become dangerous recently — bandits or possibly Shadow Cult agents have been attacking supply carts. Willowmere supplies Ashenmoor with grain, vegetables, and livestock.

## The Shadow Caves

An abandoned mine complex north of Ashenmoor. The Guild sealed the entrances fifty years ago after miners broke through into a natural cavern system filled with Shadowtouched rock — stone that absorbs light and radiates cold. Recently, the seals have been broken. The Shadow Cult is suspected.

Strange creatures have been seen emerging from the caves at night — shapes made of darkness that dissolve in sunlight. Grenn's apprentice, Tomas, was last seen heading toward the Shadow Caves three weeks ago.
