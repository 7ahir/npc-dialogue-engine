# Notable Items and Materials

## Starfall Steel

A legendary alloy forged from meteoric iron and refined aether crystals. Starfall Steel is stronger than any conventional metal and conducts magical energy. Weapons made from Starfall Steel can cut through magically reinforced armor and materials that resist normal blades — including dragon scales.

Forging Starfall Steel requires three components: meteoric iron (extremely rare), Moonstone Ingots (for the aether crystalline structure), and a forge hot enough to melt both (the forges of Ashenmoor are among the few that qualify). Only a master smith with knowledge of the old techniques can work the material. Grenn Ironheart is one of perhaps five living smiths who know the process.

The last known Starfall Steel weapon was forged eighty years ago by Grenn's grandfather, Mordek Ironheart, for Lord Commander Vyra of the Northern Watch. That blade, called Dawnfang, was lost when Vyra fell defending the Frostpeak Pass.

## Moonstone

A translucent, pale blue mineral found only in the deepest sections of the Frozen Mines. Moonstone resonates with residual aether from the damaged Ley Network. In its raw form, it glows faintly in darkness and hums at a frequency just below human hearing.

Moonstone Ingots are created by slow-heating raw Moonstone in a process that takes seven days. The heating must be precise — too hot and the aether crystalline structure collapses, leaving worthless slag. Too cold and the resonance frequency shifts, making the ingot unstable.

Unstable Moonstone is extremely dangerous. It can amplify and redirect ambient aether uncontrollably. The Sundering-era records describe Moonstone weapons detonating when exposed to concentrated magical energy. This is why Eldris insists that anyone working with Moonstone understand its resonance properties.

The Shadow Cult has been purchasing raw Moonstone through intermediaries. Their purpose is unknown, but Eldris theorizes they may be attempting to build an aether amplifier — a device that could forcibly widen the breach between the physical world and the Shadow Plane.

## The Codex Umbra

An ancient text housed in the lowest level of the Ashenmoor Archive. Written by an unknown Veilborn scholar before the Sundering, the Codex describes the theory and practice of Shadow Plane manipulation. It is the most comprehensive surviving text on Shadow magic.

The Codex is bound in black leather that is warm to the touch. Its pages are made of a material that is neither paper nor parchment. The text shifts and rearranges when read by different people, presenting different information to different readers based on their knowledge and intent.

A page from the Codex has recently gone missing. Eldris discovered the theft during a routine inspection. The missing page describes the Convergence Ritual — the same ritual the Veilborn attempted during the Veiled War. Without that page, the ritual cannot be performed correctly. With it, someone with enough Moonstone and access to a ley line junction could attempt to complete what the Sundering interrupted.

## Aether Crystals

Naturally occurring crystalline formations found at ley line junctions. Aether crystals store and channel magical energy. The Shattered Spire contains several intact crystals from the pre-Sundering Ley Network, which are far more powerful than naturally occurring ones.

Small aether crystals are used by scholars and craftspeople for minor magical tasks: lighting, preservation, communication over short distances. Large crystals — the kind found at relay stations — can power wards, maintain magical barriers, and even enable limited teleportation between connected junctions.

## Shadowtouched Rock

Stone that has been permanently altered by contact with the Shadow Plane. It appears as normal rock but absorbs light, creating patches of absolute darkness even in torchlight. It radiates cold and a faint sense of unease.

Prolonged exposure to Shadowtouched rock causes nightmares, paranoia, and in extreme cases, shadow-sickness — a condition where the afflicted begins to fade, becoming translucent, before vanishing entirely. The condition is reversible if caught early (treatment involves prolonged exposure to sunlight and aether crystal therapy), but fatal if untreated.

Shadowtouched rock is concentrated in the Shadow Caves north of Ashenmoor. The recent breaking of the Guild's seals has released shadow-sickness spores into the surrounding forest.
