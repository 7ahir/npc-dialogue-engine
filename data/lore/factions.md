# Factions of Aethermoor

## The Ashenmoor Council

The governing body of the town, composed of five elected representatives: the Mine Warden, the Trade Warden, the People's Warden, the Archive Keeper, and the Marshal. Decisions require a majority vote. The current Mine Warden, Borik Deepdelve, holds the most influence due to the town's dependence on mining revenue.

The Council is generally fair but slow to act. They have been reluctant to address the Shadow Cult threat, dismissing it as superstition. Only the Archive Keeper, Eldris the Veiled, takes the threat seriously, but they rarely attend Council meetings.

## The Miners' Guild

A powerful labor organization that controls access to the mines. Members pay dues, receive safety training, and get priority on ore contracts. The Guild Master, Halla Stonebreaker, is respected but aging. A succession dispute is brewing between her two lieutenants.

The Guild maintains the deep tunnels where Moonstone is found. They have strict rules about Moonstone extraction — it must be mined by hand, never with explosives, because of its resonance with residual aether in the rock. Guild miners report strange sounds from the deepest tunnels — rhythmic humming that matches no known geological phenomenon.

## The Shadow Cult

A secretive organization that seeks to complete the Veilborn ritual that caused the Sundering. They believe the partial merger with the Shadow Plane was not a catastrophe but a gift — one that must be completed to "reunify the fractured world." Their members include disillusioned scholars, desperate miners, and at least one member of the town Council (identity unknown).

The Cult operates through coded messages, dead drops, and nighttime meetings in abandoned mine shafts. They have been collecting Moonstone for an unknown purpose. Their leader is known only as "The Convergence" — a title, not a name.

Recent Cult activity has increased. Strange shadow-creatures have been spotted near the old mine entrances. The ley line beneath Ashenmoor has begun pulsing more strongly, which Eldris attributes to the Cult's tampering.

## The Wandering Merchants

A loose network of traders who travel between towns. They bring news, exotic goods, and occasionally trouble. The most notable is Caelum Brightpath, a half-elf who deals in magical components and has connections to every Archive on the continent.

The Merchants are not a formal faction but their collective knowledge of the world's roads and dangers makes them invaluable. They are the primary source of news for isolated towns like Ashenmoor.

## The Archive Keepers

An ancient order dedicated to preserving knowledge. They maintain Archives in major settlements across the continent. Each Keeper swears an oath of neutrality and serves for life. The Ashenmoor Archive is one of the oldest and most complete, housing texts dating back to before the Sundering.

Archive Keepers trade knowledge for knowledge — they never accept coin. They will answer any question if the asker brings information they do not already possess. This has made them both respected and resented.
